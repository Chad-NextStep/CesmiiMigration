Our Story

The journey behind America's Smart Manufacturing Institute

How It All Began

CESMII was established in 2016 as part of the Manufacturing USA network, a national initiative to secure U.S. global leadership in advanced manufacturing. Recognizing the transformative potential of Smart Manufacturing, the U.S. Department of Energy partnered with a broad coalition of industry leaders, universities and national laboratories to create an institute dedicated to accelerating SM adoption across the country.

From the very beginning, CESMII set out to democratize access to Smart Manufacturing technologies — ensuring that organizations of every size, from multinational corporations to small and medium manufacturers, could harness the power of data-driven production.

Key Milestones

Over the years CESMII has grown from a founding vision into a thriving ecosystem. Early efforts focused on building the SM Innovation Platform (SMIP), an open, standards-based technology stack that gives manufacturers a common foundation for interoperability and innovation. Since then, the institute has funded dozens of collaborative research projects, launched workforce development programs reaching thousands of professionals, and expanded its membership to include hundreds of organizations across the manufacturing value chain.

Each milestone reflects CESMII's commitment to turning cutting-edge research into practical, shop-floor impact — bridging the gap between what is possible in the lab and what is achievable in everyday manufacturing.

Leadership & Governance

CESMII is guided by a diverse leadership team and governed by a board of directors drawn from industry, academia and government. This cross-sector governance model ensures that the institute's strategy remains grounded in real-world manufacturing needs while pushing the boundaries of innovation.

Our leadership brings together decades of experience in manufacturing operations, information technology, public policy and academic research — a combination that enables CESMII to tackle the complex, multidisciplinary challenges of Smart Manufacturing.
